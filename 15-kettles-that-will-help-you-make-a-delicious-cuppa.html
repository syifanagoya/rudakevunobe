HOW DO I CHOOSE A KETTLE?

There are a few factors to take into consideration when choosing your perfect kettle.

• Budget: A smaller budget may get you a model with fewer features, but it will still deliver boiling water.

• Style: Would you prefer a traditional stovetop model, or an electric kettle?

• Capacity: If you’re frequently entertaining, a larger model will allow you to serve up multiple cuppas at a time. But if you’re short on space, a smaller model may be ideal.

• Features: Spend a little more, and you can get high-quality features – including quiet boil, adjustable temperatures, and ergonomic designs.

• Aesthetics: Let’s face it – looks matter. Retro style kettles from brands like Smeg are a little more expensive, but their vintage-chic style is very on-trend and will brighten up your kitchen.

HOW OFTEN SHOULD YOU CHANGE YOUR KETTLE?

A kettle should last you between four and six years, so if it’s been that long, consider upgrading. You should also upgrade as soon as your kettle begins to show any signs of serious wear or malfunction, like rusting or excessive scaling.

HOW DO I CLEAN A KETTLE?

They may only be used for boiling water, but kettles can get grimy and require cleaning in order to keep them in top-notch quality for as long as possible. Products like the Breville Kettle Cleaner ($8.95 at Amazon Australia) are a super effective and easy way to remove scale build-up and stains and will give your kettle a new lease on life.

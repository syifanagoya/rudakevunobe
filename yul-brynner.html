What ethnicity is Yul Brynner, Spouse, With Hair, Wikipedia, Nationality, Cause of Death – Yul Brynner, born on July 11, 1920, in Vladivostok, Russia, began life as Yuliy Borisovich Briner. His parents were Boris Yuliyevich Briner, a mining engineer, and Marousia Dimitrievna. Yul’s childhood took an unexpected turn when his father’s extramarital affair led him and his sister into the care of their mother. They journeyed from China to France in 1932, where Yul’s journey into the world of arts and entertainment began.

Yul Brynner Early Life and Career

In Paris, Brynner’s musical inclination flourished, and he took up the guitar, playing in Russian nightclubs. A brief stint as a trapeze acrobat in a French circus ended due to a back injury. This setback, however, propelled him toward a new career—acting.

In 1940, Yul and his mother migrated to the United States, settling in New York City. During World War II, he worked as a French-speaking radio announcer, broadcasting propaganda to occupied France. This period also saw his foray into acting, thanks to training under Russian coach Michael Chekhov.

Yul Brynner Turning Point

Initially hesitant to pursue acting, Brynner’s life took a significant turn when he auditioned for the role of ‘King Mongkut’ in the musical “The King and I” in 1947. This decision marked the beginning of an extraordinary career. He played the role a staggering 4,625 times on stage, earning critical acclaim and two Tony Awards.

The success of “The King and I” catapulted him into stardom, leading to roles in films like “The Ten Commandments” and “Anastasia.” His distinctive shaved head, adopted for the role, became his personal trademark.

Yul Brynner Filmography

Brynner’s film career flourished with roles in “The Magnificent Seven,” “Taras Bulba,” and “Solomon and Sheba.” His versatility shone in diverse genres, from epic dramas to Westerns. Notably, he won an Academy Award for Best Actor for his portrayal of ‘King Mongkut’ in the film adaptation of “The King and I” in 1956.

Apart from acting, Brynner explored other creative pursuits. He directed, modeled, and even played the guitar. His musical talents manifested in an album titled “The Gypsy and I: Yul Brynner Sings Gypsy Songs,” released in 1967.

Yul Brynner Books and Photography

Yul Brynner wasn’t confined to the screen; he ventured into photography and writing. Throughout his life, he penned books, including “Bring Forth the Children: A Journey to the Forgotten People of Europe and the Middle East” and “The Yul Brynner Cookbook: Food Fit for the King and You.” These books showcased his photography skills, offering glimpses into his unique perspective.

Yul Brynner Awards and Recognition

Brynner’s contributions to the entertainment industry earned him accolades. In 1952, he received a Tony Award for Best Featured Actor in a Musical for “The King and I.” His exceptional dedication to the role also earned him a special Tony Award.

The pinnacle of his recognition came in 1956 when he clinched the Academy Award for Best Actor for the film adaptation of “The King and I.” His star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame at 6162 Hollywood Blvd stands as a testament to his enduring impact.

Yul Brynner Personal Life

Yul Brynner’s personal life was as colorful as his professional journey. He married four times, starting with actress Virginia Gilmore in 1944. This union lasted until 1960 and bore a son, Rock Yul Brynner. His second marriage, with Doris Kleiner, lasted from 1960 to 1967, resulting in a daughter, Victoria Brynner.

His third marriage, to Jacqueline Thion de la Chaume, lasted from 1971 to 1981, and the couple adopted two children, Mia and Melody. In 1983, he married Kathy Lee, concluding with his death in 1985.

Brynner battled health issues, particularly his struggle with smoking. After quitting in 1971, he was diagnosed with inoperable lung cancer in 1983. Despite radiation therapy, he succumbed to lung cancer on October 10, 1985, in New York City.

His legacy lives on through the Yul Brynner Park, where a statue was erected in his honor on September 28, 2012. The cottage of his childhood in Sidimi, near Vladivostok, now serves as a family museum, preserving the memories of a man who left an indelible mark on the world of entertainment.

What Happened to Yul Brynner?

Yul Brynner passed away on October 10, 1985, at the age of 65. The cause of his death was lung cancer. He died at New York Hospital, and following his wishes, his remains were cremated. His ashes found their resting place in the grounds of the Saint-Michel-de-Bois-Aubry Orthodox monastery, near Luzé, France, located between Tours and Poitiers.

Who is Yul Brynner’s Son?

Yul Brynner’s son is Rock Brynner. Both his parents, Yul Brynner and Virginia Gilmore, were actors. Rock Brynner’s life journey took him from a childhood in the United States and Switzerland to a diverse career that unfolded from Broadway to Vladivostok.

Who did Yul Brynner leave his estate to?

When Yul Brynner passed away, the majority of his estate went to his fourth wife, Kathy Brynner. As part of the inheritance, she received their New York co-op apartment at United Nations Plaza. Kathy later sold the apartment approximately three years later for around $1.75 million.
